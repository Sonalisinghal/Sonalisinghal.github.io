Open Source

Here is a list of some of the open source programs that happen every year. participating in them is a great way to expand your skillset. Know that its okay even if you have never contributed before. Its never too late to start learning! :)

Some tips to start contributing:

  1. Find your passion, interests and technologies that you are interested in.
  2. From the list of projects, pick the ones that you find exciting.
  3. Find the communication channels that the organisation uses. For example IRC, Slack, Gitter, Mailing list etc
  4. Introduce yourself to the community, let them know you're interested. Open source organisations are generally very welcoming to new contributors.
  5. Find newcomer-friendly bugs/features, things that you can work on to get familiar with the codebase.
  6. If you are unable to find anything, talk to the project maintainers or the mentors. They can help you find a suitable task.
  7. Integrate into the community. Help others, be regular, give updates about your work.
  8. If you plan to do a summer of code/internship with an organisation, then definitely discuss your ideas with the mentor.
  9. Every organisation is different. Do not fret if you don't get replies to your messages quickly. Mentors can be busy! Do not message them repeatedly. Make an honest attempt to solve your problem before asking doubts.

Outreachy

Outreachy provides internships to work in open source and free software. Outreachy internships are open to applicants around the world. Interns work remotely, and are not required to move.

Applications Open: February and September
Applications Close : March and October respectively
Eligibiblity: Women Cis-Trans, residents and nationals of the United States of any gender who are Black/African American, Hispanic/Latin, Native American/American Indian, Alaska Native, Native Hawaiian, or Pacific Islander.
Stipend: $5000

GirlsScript Summer of Code

Eligibiblity: Everyone

Rails Girls Summer of Code

Rails Girls Summer of Code is a global fellowship program for women and non-binary coders. Students receive a three-month scholarship to work on existing Open Source projects and expand their skill set. Student participate in teams of 2.

Applications Open: March
Applications Close : March end
Eligibiblity: Females, non binary
Stipend: As quoted by the student, in accordance with monthly cost of living of the region

The X.Org Endless Vacation of Code (EVoC)

X.Org Endless Vacation of Code (EVoC) is a Google Summer of Code (GSoC)-like project initiated in 2008 funded by the X.Org Foundation. It allows students to participate to X. Org-related projects during their vacation, at any time of the year.

Applications Open: Anytime
Applications Close : Anytime
Eligibiblity: Anyone can apply. Stipend is only provided to students with age 18+
Stipend: $5,000
